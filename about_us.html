About ABV-IIITM Gwalior

Atal Bihari Vajpayee - Indian Institute of Information Technology and Management, Gwalior (ABV-IIITM Gwalior), is an apex Information Technology (IT) and Management Institute, established by the Government of India in 1997. The institute is recognized as a deemed-to-be-university. The Institute offers various programmes at Master’s and Doctoral level. Sprawling across 160 lush green acres on foothills of the Gwalior Fort, the Institute is self-contained with basic amenities for all round development of the students. The Institute’s activities are aimed at developing amongst students an inquisitive mind and a culture of camaraderie and research.

Infrastructure

ABV-IIITM Gwalior is a fully residential institute with 160 acres land, 3 boys’ hostels, 1 girls’ hostel and faculty accommodation. Institute has a well furnished guest house with 35 rooms. Institute has 5 academic blocks. All the lecture and tutorial rooms are centrally air conditioned and are fully equipped with contemporary technological teaching aids for effective delivery. Institute has 18 research/teaching labs.

Academics

ABV-IIITM Gwalior is predominantly a research led postgraduate institute. Institute offers four academic programs: Ph.D., Master of Technology (M. Tech.), Master of Business Administration (MBA) and Integrated Postgraduate Program (IPG). The admission procedure is very competitive; only top students are admitted as per the merit obtained in a national level competitive examination for entrance in engineering institutions. Institute has 28 full time faculty members; all are Ph.D. Institute subscribes print and online journals and data bases, e.g., IEEE, Science Direct, Springer series, EBSCO, Emerald. Institute has networking and collaboration with various International/national institutes of world repute. Institute has a very good linkage with other national institutes in the city too, e.g., Defense Research and Development Establishment (DRDE), Lakshmi Bai National University for Physical education (LNUPE), Indian Institute of Tourism and Travel Management (IITTM). Faculty members are actively engaged in the sponsored research projects related to the mandate of the institute.

About Gwalior

Gwalior, a city of state Madhya Pradesh, India is known for its educational institutes, music, history and art. The city is proud of its ancient culture blended with modern outlook. Gwalior houses one of the oldest and most beautiful forts in India, known as “The Gwalior Fort”. Five universities and many national/state level educational institutes make Gwalior an educational hub of Madhya Pradesh. Being centrally located on the geographical map of India, the city is well connected to all the major cities of Indian by road, rail and air. The city is on the world map of airlines and boasts of very good connectivity with the political capital of India, New Delhi, and with financial capital of India, Mumbai, on daily basis. Gwalior has one 5 star and many 3 star hotels. One of the Seven Wonders of the World, TAJ MAHAL is 123 Kms away from Gwalior. Khajuraho, the other most popular tourist destinations of India, which is known for its erotic sculpture, is 275 Kms away from Gwalior. Orcha, 130 Kms from Gwalior, encompasses magnificent historical monuments and Hindu architecture. National Chambal Sanctuary, just 30 Kms away from Gwalior, protects the pristine river ecosystem and harbours a variety of aquatic life like the elusive Ganges River Dolphin, Crocodile (Muggar), Gharial (Gavelia Gangeticus), Freshwater Turtles, River Otters and a various species of fishes.
